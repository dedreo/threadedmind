ChatGPT said:
Yeah, the "Ouroboros Effect" (or model collapse) is a real concern in AI development—especially with large language models like me.

The gist of it is this:

As more content on the internet is generated by AI, future AI models trained on that content start learning from AI-written outputs, rather than human-created data.

Over time, this leads to reinforced mediocrity—models mimic patterns generated by previous models, amplifying inaccuracies, clichés, and stylistic sameness.

It's named after the mythic Ouroboros, the serpent that eats its own tail—a cycle of self-consumption.

Researchers have shown that continual training on synthetic data lowers model diversity and accuracy, causing what they call model degeneration. It's a big reason OpenAI and others put a premium on curating high-quality, human-origin data and developing techniques to filter synthetic content from training corpora.

It's also a reason human creativity, diversity, and expression remain critical—not just culturally, but now existentially, for tech.

Want to dig into the research that discovered this effect?
